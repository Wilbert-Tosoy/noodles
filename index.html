Pancit Canton

The most often consumed noodle dish in Filipino households and restaurants. This meal has egg noodles stir-fried with vegetables like as carrots, green beans, shitake mushrooms, and cabbage, as well as pieces of pork.

Pork liver and sliced fish balls are occasionally added. The dish is typically seasoned with soy sauce, oyster sauce, or a mixture of the two, with calamansi put on top for the diner to season as desired.

Pancit Palabok/Luglog

The rich, viscous sauce seasoned with shrimp and colored with atsuete(annatto seeds)Â distinguishes this rice noodle dish.

Steamed shrimp, spring onions, crisp fried chunks of smoked milkfish, and crumbled chicharon(fried pork rinds) are then added to the noodles . Calamansi is again put on top for the diner to season the meal.

Lomi

Known as "braised noodles" in Chinese, lomi features thick egg noodles (similar to Japanese udon but with egg) and is braised in a thick broth with carrots, cabbage, and mushrooms, generally with pieces of pork, shrimp, kikiam(pork and taro wrapped in tofu skin), and other vegetables.

Some variants include an egg in the thick soup. The province of Batangas is well-known for its Batangas Lomi.

La Paz Batchoy

This is the crowning achievement of Iloilo cuisine. Egg noodles are served with fried pork skin, braised hog flesh, liver and intestine, and cattle bone marrow in a delicious broth prepared from pork and beef. Just before serving, fried minced garlic is also added.

Albondingas (Misua Meatball Soup)

While pancit is influenced by Chinese cuisine, creative Filipinos mixed misua (angel hair flour noodles) with the Spanish dish albondigas (meaning meatballs) to create this Filipino delicacy.

Misua noodles are cooked with pig meatballs in a broth. Meanwhile, a shrimp and patola (sponge gourd or loofah) variation is popular in Filipino families, especially during the rainy season.

Mami

The name is derived from a Chinese term that means "pork noodles," however variants of this dish are typically made with beef or chicken.

Egg (or flour) noodles are served with a savory broth prepared from beef or chicken bones, as well as napa cabbage pieces, hardboiled egg, and shredded chicken or beef. Some varieties include wontons.

Pancit Habhab

Stir-fried egg noodles with pork, carrots, cabbage, and chayote, seasoned with soy sauce and/or oyster sauce. Unlike pancit canton, these noodles should be seasoned with vinegar rather than calamansi.

This is generally served without utensils on a banana leaf. Simply place the banana leaf packaging in your mouth and suck the noodles!

If you want to enjoy true pancit habhab, you need go to Lucban, Quezon, where it originated and is produced from scratch.

Pancit Choco En Su Tinta

A traditional Cavite dish also known as "Pancit Pusit." Unlike any other Filipino noodle meal, the rice noodles are cooked with squid and squid ink, giving the noodles a stunning black hue.

It has cabbage, carrots, chiles, kinchay (Chinese celery), and chicharon on top. When kamias fruit (bimbing) is in season, it is added to the noodles to give them a spicy taste.

Kinalas

Almost like a beef mami, the egg noodles in this form, which is predominantly found in the Bicol area, are served with a rich beef broth created from braising sections of the cow head until soft or coming off the bone, as the name indicates.

It is typically served with a hard-boiled egg and a strange, enigmatic brown sauce, the recipe and components of which are kept secret by most sellers.

Pancit Henoy

This Cavite noodle dish, named "Henoy" after national hero General Emilio Aguinaldo, is considered to be his favorite. In a soup containing shrimp, calf livers, white and napa cabbage, patola, and glutinous rice flour, egg and rice noodles are cooked.

While it is wonderful, it may be tough to find because restaurants seldom serve it.

Pancit Batil Patung

The somewhat thicker egg noodles in this dish, a delicacy of Northern Luzon areas like Tuguegarao, are customarily stir-fried with minced carabeef (buffalo meat), beansprouts, and spring onions. Most variations include a fried or poached egg on top.

On the side is a rich brown sauce with a beaten egg, which is then poured over the noodles. The noodles are then seasoned with a mixture of chopped onions, soy sauce, vinegar, calamansi, and chilies.

Pancit Puso

Pancit Puso is another Cavite noodle dish. A blend of egg and rice noodles is cooked with atsuete-colored pork pieces, cabbage, carrots, and green beans. The banana blossoms are then simmered in vinegar and added to the meal, imparting a bright, zesty taste to the noodles while also providing another textural aspect.
